Curated Reading List

Welcome to my database for social good research. This is where I save papers that I am currently reading or going to read, as well as brief commentary on how the paper does (or does not) align with my goals and interests. Specifically, there are three high-level questions governing my research directions:

  • Bottlenecks: Given any goal towards social/environmental impact, be it women's health, wildfire mitigation, decreasing wealth inequality, and more, what is/are the leading bottleneck(s) preventing positive change? I suspect that most bottlenecks can be classified into one of two categories: limitations in public policy or technological shortcomings; these categories lead to my next two questions.

  • Public Policy: For a specific social good goal, if public policy is the bottleneck preventing change, how can the algorithmically and statistically inclined use technology to empower representatives in making their case for changes to social/economic infrastructure?

  • Deployable Models: If public policy is not the bottleneck preventing change, then how do we close the gap between what is technologically needed and what is currently possible?
